Workshop on Safety Analysis for Autonomous System: Practical Perspectives and Recent Advances

December 15-18, 2026

Hilton Hawaiian Village in Honolulu, Hawaii, USA.

65th IEEE Conference on Decision and Control

The deployment of autonomous systems in the real world — from self-driving vehicles to unmanned aerial systems — requires rigorous methods for reasoning about whether a system can maintain safety, broadly understood as the ability to enforce constraints on system behavior. Safety is among the most fundamental challenges in control theory and a prerequisite for deploying autonomous systems in the real world. Two prominent families of methods have emerged to address this challenge: Control Barrier Functions (CBFs) and Hamilton-Jacobi (HJ) Reachability Analysis. While two frameworks share the overarching goal of performing safety analysis for control systems, they are rooted in distinct mathematical foundations — CBFs in Lyapunov stability theory and set invariance, HJ reachability in optimal control theory and dynamic programming — and hence offer different insights into the problem of safety analysis and lend themselves to different analysis and design tools. They also shared common open challenges, such as scalability to high-dimensional systems and integration with control systems with learned components. This workshop focuses on CBFs and HJ reachability as prominent representatives of the broader landscape of safety-critical control and aims to bring together researchers working across this space to exchange ideas and accelerate progress towards the safe deployment of autonomous systems.

Call for Papers

We invite researchers and practitioners to submit their papers to the workshop to be included in the lightning talks. Submissions should address topics related to safety in autonomous systems, including but not limited to:

  • Robustness against adversarial perturbations
  • Verifiability of decision-making models
  • Interpretability in high-stakes environments
  • Formal methods in certifying autonomous systems
